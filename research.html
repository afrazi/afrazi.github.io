Enhancing Offshore Wind Farm Efficiency Through Wake Dynamics

Wake interactions in offshore wind farms, caused by aerodynamic disturbances between turbines, represent a critical challenge to maximizing energy efficiency. These interactions reduce power output and increase mechanical stress on downstream turbines, leading to significant losses in overall performance. The understanding and mitigation of these effects are essential for advancing the sustainability and reliability of wind energy systems. In this study, a comprehensive sensitivity analysis of key operational and environmental parameters was conducted to examine their influence on wake dynamics. Factors such as turbulence intensity, yaw misalignment, and turbine operational characteristics were analyzed using advanced modeling techniques. The motivation behind this work stems from the need to address the inefficiencies that arise from wake effects in clustered turbine arrangements, which are becoming increasingly prevalent as offshore wind farms expand. The novelty of this research lies in its approach to integrating experimental data with modeling frameworks, enabling the calibration of predictive tools that reflect real-world conditions. Seasonal variations in turbulence intensity and their impact on wake recovery and power losses were a particular focus, shedding light on the temporal dynamics of offshore wind farm performance. The findings provide valuable insights into optimizing turbine layout and operational strategies to minimize energy losses caused by wake effects. By improving the accuracy of wake modeling and understanding the interplay of key parameters, this work contributes to enhancing the efficiency and reliability of offshore wind farms, aligning with global efforts to expand renewable energy capacity.
